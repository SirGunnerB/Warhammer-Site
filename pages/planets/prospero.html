Overview

Prospero was once a shining beacon of psychic and cultural achievement, home to the Thousand Sons Legion and their Primarch Magnus the Red. Now it stands as a dead world, its libraries burned and its crystal spires shattered during the infamous Burning of Prospero.

History

Pre-Imperial Era

Founded as a refuge for psykers fleeing persecution. Developed into a center of psychic and academic learning.

Discovery (ca. M30)

The Emperor discovers Magnus the Red on Prospero. The planet becomes the home of the Thousand Sons Legion.

Golden Age

Prospero reaches its zenith as a center of psychic and academic learning. The Great Library of Tizca becomes one of the greatest repositories of knowledge in the galaxy.

Council of Nikaea

The Emperor restricts the use of psychic powers. Magnus continues his studies in secret.

The Warning

Magnus attempts to warn the Emperor of Horus's betrayal through sorcery, inadvertently damaging the Imperial Webway Project.

The Burning (734.M30)

Space Wolves, Sisters of Silence, and Custodian Guard attack Prospero. The planet is razed and its libraries destroyed.

Post-Heresy

Prospero becomes a dead world, occasionally visited by those seeking forbidden knowledge in its ruins.

City of Tizca

The Great Pyramid

  • Center of Thousand Sons operations
  • Magnus's personal sanctuary
  • Repository of forbidden knowledge
  • Training grounds for psychic arts

Crystal Spires

Tizca was known as the City of Light, its crystal spires reaching into the sky, each housing different schools of psychic and academic study.

The Great Library

  • Vast collection of psychic lore
  • Ancient texts from across the galaxy
  • Psychically-preserved knowledge
  • Forbidden grimoires

Society & Culture

Academic Focus

  • Emphasis on scholarly pursuit
  • Preservation of knowledge
  • Psychic development
  • Artistic expression

Psychic Traditions

Prospero's society was built around the development and study of psychic powers, with different schools focusing on various aspects of psychic mastery.

Cultural Achievement

  • Advanced architecture
  • Psychic art forms
  • Scientific advancement
  • Philosophical schools

The Burning

The Attack

  • Space Wolves Legion deployment
  • Sisters of Silence nullifying psychic powers
  • Custodian Guard elite forces
  • Orbital bombardment

The Battle

The battle for Prospero was devastating, with the Space Wolves systematically destroying the city of Tizca while the Sisters of Silence neutralized psychic defenses.

The Aftermath

  • Total destruction of Tizca
  • Loss of invaluable knowledge
  • Magnus's pact with Tzeentch
  • Thousand Sons' exodus

Legacy

Impact on the Imperium

  • Symbol of the dangers of unrestricted psychic power
  • Loss of irreplaceable knowledge
  • Creation of bitter enmity between Space Wolves and Thousand Sons

Current State

Prospero remains a dead world, its ruins still containing fragments of its former glory. The planet is occasionally visited by those seeking forbidden knowledge or artifacts of power.

Quick Facts

  • Former Population: ~100 million

  • Status: Destroyed
  • Current Inhabitants: None (occasional scavengers)
