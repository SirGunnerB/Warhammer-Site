Ollanius Pius

Imperial Guard/Perpetual Martyred Imperium

Overview

Ollanius Pius is a legendary figure in Imperial history, known for his ultimate sacrifice during the final battle between the Emperor and Horus. His story has become a symbol of mortal courage in the face of overwhelming odds, and his act of defiance against the Arch-Traitor Horus has inspired countless Imperial citizens for millennia.

While various accounts of his nature exist - from a simple Imperial Army soldier to an immortal Perpetual - all versions agree on the fundamental truth of his sacrifice and its significance to the Imperium of Man. His legend exemplifies the courage of ordinary humans in the face of godlike beings and cosmic horrors.

History

Ancient Origins

According to some accounts, Ollanius was born in ancient Terra's Franc region, around M15. As a Perpetual, he lived through countless ages of human history, witnessing humanity's greatest triumphs and darkest moments. He fought in numerous historical conflicts, from ancient battles to the World Wars of Terra's past.

Age of Strife

Survived through the collapse of human civilization during the Age of Strife, maintaining his faith in humanity despite witnessing the fall of countless human worlds and the rise of techno-barbarian states on Terra.

Unification Wars

Witnessed the Emperor's rise to power and the unification of Terra. Despite his immortal nature, chose to serve as a common soldier, believing in the importance of fighting alongside ordinary humans.

Great Crusade

Served in the Imperial Army during the Great Crusade, deliberately choosing to remain anonymous despite his ancient nature. Participated in numerous campaigns to reunite humanity under the Imperial banner.

Horus Heresy

When the Heresy erupted, remained loyal to the Emperor and continued fighting against the forces of Chaos. His experiences spanning millennia gave him unique insight into the true nature of the conflict.

The Final Sacrifice

During the climactic battle aboard Horus's flagship, the Vengeful Spirit, Pius stood between the wounded Emperor and the Warmaster. His act of defiance, though ending in his death, is said to have given the Emperor the resolve needed for the final confrontation.
