Overview

Alpharius and Omegon were unique among the Primarchs, being twins who shared a single Legion. Their very existence as twins was one of the most closely guarded secrets of the Imperium, with even their brother Primarchs unaware of Omegon's existence. Masters of subterfuge and deception, their true loyalties and current status remain one of the greatest mysteries of the Horus Heresy.

In the 41st millennium, the Alpha Legion continues to operate in cells across the galaxy, and reports of Alpharius or Omegon leading operations persist, though the truth of these claims remains impossible to verify.

Early Life

The Twin Primarchs

Unlike their brother Primarchs, Alpharius and Omegon's early history remains largely unknown, possibly even to themselves. Their unique nature as twins may have been an intentional creation by the Emperor, or an unexpected outcome of the Primarch Project. What is known is that they operated in perfect synchronization from the moment of their discovery.

Discovery

Officially, Alpharius was the last Primarch to be discovered, though accounts of his finding vary wildly and may be intentionally misleading. Some sources suggest he was discovered by Horus himself, while others indicate he may have been operating within the Imperium long before his "official" discovery.

Great Crusade

Leading the Alpha Legion

Under their leadership, the Alpha Legion developed unprecedented expertise in covert operations, infiltration, and psychological warfare. Their methods often drew criticism from other Legions, but their effectiveness was undeniable. The twins' ability to seamlessly switch roles and maintain multiple concurrent operations became legendary.

Methods and Philosophy

The Alpha Legion's approach to warfare emphasized intelligence gathering, infiltration, and the manipulation of enemy forces. They frequently employed human operatives and complex networks of agents, demonstrating a unique appreciation for baseline humanity among the Legions.

The Horus Heresy

The Cabal's Influence

Their involvement with the mysterious xenos organization known as the Cabal proved pivotal in their decision to seemingly support Horus. The Cabal presented them with a vision of two possible futures: the Emperor's victory leading to ten thousand years of stagnation, or Horus's victory causing humanity's extinction and denying Chaos its power source.

Complex Loyalties

Evidence suggests the twins may have worked toward different goals during the Heresy, with some sources indicating Omegon may have secretly supported the Emperor while Alpharius followed the Cabal's plan. Their true motivations and actions during this period remain debated by Imperial scholars.

Post-Heresy

Conflicting Reports

Alpharius was reportedly killed by Rogal Dorn during the Battle of Pluto, though given the Legion's predilection for deception, this remains uncertain. Omegon's fate is entirely unknown, with some suggesting he assumed the identity of Alpharius after his brother's death.

Legacy of Deception

The Alpha Legion continues to operate in the 41st millennium, maintaining their founders' traditions of subterfuge and multiple, concurrent operations. Their true allegiance remains unclear, with different cells seemingly working both for and against Imperial interests.

Legacy

The legacy of Alpharius and Omegon is one of uncertainty and complexity. Their influence extends far beyond their apparent allegiances, with their methods and philosophy continuing to impact Imperial and Chaos operations alike. The Alpha Legion's continued activities suggest their grand strategy may still be in motion.

Impact on Warfare

  • Revolution in covert warfare techniques
  • Development of cell-based resistance networks
  • Integration of human agents in Astartes operations
  • Advancement of infiltration technology
