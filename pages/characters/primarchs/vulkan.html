Overview

Vulkan, the Lord of Drakes, was the Primarch of the Salamanders Legion. Known for his superhuman strength, mastery of forgecraft, and most importantly, his compassion for ordinary humans, Vulkan embodied the humanitarian ideals of the Emperor's vision. His unique perpetual nature made him essentially immortal, able to return to life after death.

Unlike many of his brother Primarchs, Vulkan placed great emphasis on the protection of civilian lives during military operations, a philosophy that would define the Salamanders' approach to warfare. His skill as a craftsman was legendary, creating numerous artifacts of incredible power that would later become revered relics of his Legion.

Early Life

Life on Nocturne

Landing on the harsh world of Nocturne, Vulkan was found and raised by N'bel, a blacksmith of the city-state of Hesiod. From an early age, he showed remarkable abilities in craftsmanship and combat. His most famous early deed was slaying a great salamander drake, earning him his name and the respect of his people.

Rise to Leadership

Vulkan's rise to leadership came through the traditional contest of the Salamander's Challenge, where he not only succeeded in killing the largest drake ever seen but also saved many lives in the process. This event demonstrated both his martial prowess and his dedication to protecting others, traits that would define his leadership style.

Great Crusade

Leading the Salamanders

When reunited with the Emperor, Vulkan took command of the XVIII Legion, renaming them the Salamanders. Under his leadership, the Legion became known for their mastery of artificer weapons and armor, their tactical use of flame weapons, and their unwavering dedication to protecting civilian populations during military operations.

Brotherhood with Primarchs

During the Great Crusade, Vulkan formed close bonds with several of his brother Primarchs, particularly Ferrus Manus, with whom he shared a passion for crafting. His relationship with his brothers was characterized by his steady temperament and wisdom, often serving as a voice of reason and compassion.

The Horus Heresy

Isstvan V and Beyond

Vulkan was present at the Drop Site Massacre on Isstvan V, where he was believed killed but survived due to his perpetual nature. He was subsequently captured by Night Lords Primarch Konrad Curze, who subjected him to repeated torture and death in an attempt to break his spirit.

Recovery and Return

After escaping Curze, Vulkan eventually made his way to Macragge, though his experiences had left him mentally unstable. Through the efforts of his sons and the artifact known as the Fulgurite, he was eventually restored to his right mind and rejoined the war effort.

Legacy

Vulkan's influence on the Imperium extends far beyond his martial achievements. His emphasis on protecting civilian lives and his humanitarian approach to warfare continue to influence the Salamanders Chapter's doctrine. The artifacts he created, known as the Artifacts of Vulkan, became legendary relics sought by his sons.

His mysterious disappearance during the War of the Beast has led many to believe he may return in humanity's darkest hour, as he had done so many times before. The Salamanders maintain that their Primarch lives and will return when needed most.
