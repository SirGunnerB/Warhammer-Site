The Enslaver Plague

Catastrophic Event Psychic Invasion Historical Event

Overview

The Enslaver Plague marked one of the most catastrophic events in galactic history, occurring during the final phases of the War in Heaven. As the conflict between the Old Ones and Necrons reached its peak, the increasing psychic presence in the galaxy created tears in reality, allowing Enslavers and other Warp entities to pour into realspace en masse.

The Enslavers

Nature

Enslavers were powerful Warp entities capable of dominating the minds of psychic beings. Unlike demons, they were native to the Warp itself rather than manifestations of emotions or desires.

Capabilities

  • Psychic domination
  • Reality breach creation
  • Mass possession
  • Psychic networking

The Outbreak

Initial Signs

  • Reality tears
  • Psychic disturbances
  • Mass possessions
  • Warp storms

Escalation

As the Old Ones created more psychically-active races to combat the Necrons, they inadvertently provided the Enslavers with more vectors for invasion. Each possessed psyker became a living portal for more Enslavers to enter realspace.

The Spread

Infection Pattern

  • Initial breaches
  • Psychic domination
  • Portal creation
  • Exponential growth

Impact on Civilizations

The plague spread rapidly through psychically active populations, with each infected individual becoming a gateway for more Enslavers. Entire worlds fell as their populations were dominated and transformed into living portals.

Consequences

Immediate Effects

  • Mass extinction of psychic races
  • Fall of the Old Ones
  • Disruption of the War in Heaven
  • Galactic depopulation

Long-Term Impact

  • Transformation of the Warp
  • Evolution of surviving species
  • Changes in psychic potential
  • Creation of new defense mechanisms

Faction Responses

The Old Ones

  • Attempted containment
  • Creation of psychic barriers
  • Emergency evacuation
  • Ultimate failure

The Necrons

  • Withdrawal from affected regions
  • Implementation of the Great Sleep
  • Development of anti-psychic technology
  • Preservation protocols

Created Races

  • Aeldari adaptation
  • Evolution of defense mechanisms
  • Cultural transformations
  • Survival strategies

Legacy

Modern Era Impact

The Enslaver Plague's effects continue to influence galactic development. Many of the defenses and protocols developed during this period still protect against Warp incursions in the 41st Millennium.

Surviving Knowledge

  • Necron records
  • Aeldari myths
  • Archaeological evidence
  • Defensive technologies

Modern Implications

Current Threats

While Enslavers still exist in the 41st Millennium, their numbers and influence are greatly reduced. However, the threat they represent has shaped many of the Imperium's anti-psyker protocols and the Black Ships' purpose.

Preventive Measures

  • Psychic screening
  • Sanctioning processes
  • Containment protocols
  • Emergency procedures
