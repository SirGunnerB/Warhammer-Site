The Nightbringer

C'tan Star God Death and Destruction Sharded

Overview

The Nightbringer, known as Aza'gorod in the Necron tongue, is perhaps the most feared of all C'tan. It is the embodiment of death itself, and its influence is so profound that it has shaped how most species in the galaxy perceive death - as a dark, skeletal reaper of souls. This cultural imprint is known as the "Reaper of Souls" effect.

Manifestation

Physical Appearance

The Nightbringer appears as a towering, skeletal figure wrapped in tattered darkness. It carries a massive scythe that can harvest the souls of entire worlds. Even in its sharded form, it maintains this terrifying visage, though at a reduced scale.

Domain

The Nightbringer's influence extends over death, darkness, and destruction. Its very presence can cause intense fear and despair in mortal beings, a result of its ancient imprint on their species' psyche.

Symbols

  • The Scythe of Death

Historical Impact

War in Heaven

During the War in Heaven, the Nightbringer was one of the most destructive forces in the galaxy. It fed not just on the energy of dying stars, but on the death of countless billions, sowing terror throughout the galaxy.

The Great Sleep

After the Necron rebellion against the C'tan, the Nightbringer was shattered into shards, though its influence remained. These shards were imprisoned in tesseract labyrinths across numerous tomb worlds.

Cultural Impact

The Reaper Effect

The Nightbringer's influence is so profound that almost every species in the galaxy envisions death as a dark, skeletal reaper. This universal cultural imprint is one of its most lasting legacies.

Notable Fragments

The shards of the Nightbringer remain some of the most dangerous C'tan fragments in existence. Each shard retains the being's fundamental connection to death and harvest.
