The Deceiver

C'tan Star God Deception and Manipulation Sharded

Overview

The Deceiver, known as Mephet'ran to the Necrontyr, is among the most insidious of the C'tan. Unlike its more directly destructive kin, it prefers to work through subtle manipulation, earning its title through countless millennia of deception and betrayal. It was instrumental in convincing the Necrontyr to undergo biotransference, perhaps its greatest deception.

Manifestation

Physical Appearance

The Deceiver typically appears as a golden-skinned humanoid figure of impossible grace and beauty. However, this is merely its chosen form - it can assume any appearance at will, making its true form impossible to determine. Even its shards retain this shapeshifting ability.

Domain

The Deceiver's influence extends over lies, manipulation, and trickery. It excels at exploiting desires and fears, turning truth into lies and allies into enemies through subtle corruption.

Symbols

Historical Impact

The Great Deception

The Deceiver played a crucial role in the biotransference of the Necrontyr, convincing them that transformation into living metal would cure their afflictions. This manipulation led to the creation of the Necrons and forever changed the course of galactic history.

War in Heaven

During the War in Heaven, the Deceiver's strategies often involved turning enemies against each other, creating false alliances, and sowing discord among the Old Ones' forces.

Methods of Manipulation

Tactical Approach

The Deceiver's preferred method is to appear as a benevolent advisor or helpful deity, offering solutions to problems it often created itself. It excels at exploiting existing conflicts and creating new ones through careful manipulation.

Notable Fragments

The shards of the Deceiver are particularly dangerous due to their retention of the entity's cunning and manipulative abilities. Each shard operates independently, often working at cross-purposes with other shards.
